Visual Language (Figma-Inspired):

Clean & Approachable - Like Figma, we use generous whitespace but not sterile emptiness. The UI breathes without feeling cold. Elements have room to exist without being lost in a void, minimal soft Rounded Corners - Elements use `rounded-md` to `rounded-sm` consistently. Not too rounded to feel childish, not too square to feel harsh. Like Figma's interface - friendly but professional. Think of it as "squared-off pillows."

Typography that guides - Headers are clear and readable (semibold, proper size). Section labels are subtle but visible. We dropped the aggressive uppercase tracking in favor of natural, readable text that doesn't scream at users.

Subtle depth through shadows - Soft box shadows (`shadow-sm`, `shadow-md`) give elements a gentle lift. Color inputs and controls have a slight tint background (`bg-muted/40`) to feel touchable without being heavy.

Interactive feedback - Hover states are smooth (`transition-all`). Buttons get a slight background shift. Selected items scale slightly and get shadows. The UI responds to touch without being distracting.

Segmented controls like Figma - Tab groups use pill-style backgrounds that slide between options. Active states are highlighted with background fills, not just borders.

Visual hierarchy through grouping - Sections are clearly separated but flow naturally. Controls are grouped logically. Input fields have consistent heights and padding.

Presets that invite clicking - Gradient swatches are rounded squares that scale on hover. Selected state is obvious with rings and scale transforms.

Color palette - Warm cream background (#F3F1EE) in light mode. Pure white cards. Near-black text. Subtle borders that define without imprisoning. The accent color is used sparingly as a punctuation mark.

MOOD & AUDIENCE: Creative professionals who want power without complexity. Users who appreciate good design intuitively. People who expect things to "just work" - like using Figma, Notion, or Linear.
